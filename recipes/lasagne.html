Description

Lasagne is a classic Italian dish characterized by its layered construction. It typically consists of wide, flat pasta sheets (called lasagne noodles) that are alternated with layers of rich and flavorful fillings.

The most common fillings include a meat-based ragù (slow-cooked meat sauce), a creamy béchamel sauce, and cheese, most notably ricotta and mozzarella. Parmesan cheese is often sprinkled on top for added flavor and a golden-brown crust.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add ground beef (or meat mixture) and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned. Drain off any excess grease.
